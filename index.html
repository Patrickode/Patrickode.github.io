Hi, I'm Patrick Mitchell.

I'm a Game Design and Development Major at RIT, and though my love for games runs deep, I enjoy programming and solving problems no matter the medium. Figuring out tough design questions and finding a way to implement them is always rewarding for me, whether it's in my writing on the side, making a website look just right, or something else.

Making people happy makes me happy above all else. I cherish every opportunity I get to make something that could make the world a little brighter for someone else.

Featured Projects

Livewire Lifesaver

Platform: Unity, C#

My first submission to the Ludum Dare Compo. By the nature of the compo, everything in the game was made from the ground up, save for the player movement code, which I worked on throughout a series of One Hour Game Jams beforehand.

  • Features fully adjustable electric current system
    • Current automatically follows wires according to their order
    • Modular wire pieces that can be customized in the editor

Divinity is a fantasy story series I write in my spare time, initially conceived back in 2012. I built this website to host its chapters as I complete them.

Beat the Boss

Platform: Unity, C#

Beat the Boss is a game I made for a game jam at the University of Paderborn while studying abroad in Germany. I served as one of three programmers on our team of twelve, and the main point of communication between the designers and programmers.

  • I, along with the designers, laid out the groundwork for gameplay features, and relayed our plans to my fellow programmers
  • I programmed player attacking, rolling, and all "class" specific abilities
